A topic that I have seen appear in online discourse every now and again is the argument on whether somebody can be a "fan" of a video game without having ever played it. At first, the answer felt fairly intuitive -- no! This, however, is usually very strongly argued against. Anecdotally, the usual counterargument is something along the lines of: "I watched the gameplay! I was obsessed with the lore! I had been engaged with this fandom since I was a little kid! Are you going to say that I'm not a fan?"

Intuitively, that line of reasoning makes sense. In retrospect, I was a "fan" of Five Nights at Freddy's when I was a small kid, even though I had literally never touched any of the games. (Although I doubt I would have called myself a "fan" of it -- it was just a thing I enjoyed.)

The very first problem that arises in these discussions is that ordinary language collapses two different experiences into one: *the thing* and *the idea of the thing.*
